Overview

Survey-grade mapping from a moving platform

JAWESO's Panora and Panora PL product line captures the world as it drives — turning raw sensor streams into georeferenced 3D reality. My work was the mapping and localization pipeline at the core of the system: estimating a precise, globally-referenced trajectory and fusing every sensor into a single consistent point cloud.

The target was demanding — under 10 cm absolute positional accuracy — because the output feeds digital-twin generation and infrastructure inspection, where centimetres decide whether a measurement is trustworthy.

The Fusion Stack

Every sensor, one consistent state

  • Dual LiDAR — 2 × 640k pts/sec at 1–2 cm range accuracy for dense, high-fidelity geometry.
  • Dual RTK-GNSS — globally-referenced positioning and heading for absolute accuracy.
  • IMU + wheel odometry — high-rate motion and dead-reckoning through GNSS gaps.
  • Visual SLAM — visual odometry to constrain drift and reinforce localization.
  • Panoramic imaging — up-to-200 MP-class multi-camera capture for colourized, inspectable maps.

The hard part wasn't any single sensor — it was making them agree. I handled time synchronization across all streams and multi-camera calibration, integrated on ROS 2, so LiDAR, GNSS, IMU, and imagery share a common clock and frame.

Deliverables & Validation

Georeferenced point clouds, validated in the field

  • Georeferenced point clouds ready for digital-twin generation and infrastructure inspection.
  • Sub-10 cm absolute accuracy validated on real corridors — not just on the bench.
  • Field validation on urban and railway corridors in Germany, across varied GNSS conditions.
