Collaborative Robotic Assembly Using 3D Vision

A flexible assembly system that assembles different products directly from CAD models without reprogramming — using dual RGB-D cameras for 6-DOF pose estimation and vision-guided manipulation alongside human workers.

3D Pose Estimation CAD-to-Real Registration Multi-Camera Fusion ABB IRB 120 Intel RealSense D435i RGB-D Human-Robot Collaboration Adaptive Automation
Problem

Flexible assembly without reprogramming

Traditional robotic assembly requires dedicated programming per product variant. Changeovers are expensive and slow. The goal: a system that reads a CAD model, understands the current scene through 3D vision, and autonomously plans and executes the assembly — while sharing the workspace safely with human operators.

Architecture

CAD → perception → assembly pipeline

1 — Multi-Camera Calibration & Fusion
Dual D435i cameras calibrated and fused for wider coverage and reduced occlusion. Sensor placement optimised to maximise visible workspace geometry.
Stereo extrinsic cal → point cloud fusion → occlusion-aware placement
2 — 3D Scene Understanding & Pose Estimation
Fused depth data produces a 3D scene representation. Objects are detected and their 6-DOF poses estimated by registering observed geometry against known CAD models.
RGB-D fusion → segmentation → CAD-to-scene registration → 6-DOF pose
3 — Assembly Sequence from CAD
Assembly order and insertion strategies are derived directly from the CAD model — no per-product programming required. Changing products means loading a new CAD file.
CAD model → assembly graph → task sequence generation
4 — Vision-Guided Execution
Robot executes pick, align, and insert tasks with continuous perception updates. Vision feedback corrects alignment during insertion. Safety monitoring enables shared-workspace operation with human coworkers.
Grasp planning → servo to pose → insert with visual feedback → safety monitor
